Routine Engineering Services

  • This can be described as all engineering services which are required for the successful running of the industries, but excludes what is required on day to basis.

  • The exclusions are core production activities, floor level operational maintenance of machinery and tools, packing , forwarding etc.

  • And - “Routine” engineering is all services which is auxiliary to existing production processes, requiring engineering knowledge and skills

    • Illustrative examples of Routine Engineering services are

  • Jigs and fixtures design / Drawing

  • Lay out change

  • Cost reduction by optimisation of Manu. process

  • Quality improvement

  • Reducing of rejection

  • Technical Manual Writing

  • Manual for internal use (establishment of SOP)

    • Non-Routine Engineering Services

    Non routine engineering are those services which are required for change of product or substantial expansion or change of production process or new/substantial improvement in the product.

    Core Employability Enhancement Program

    CEEP is an intense 480 hour programme available for fresh graduates / final year engg. Students. CEEP as the name suggests is for student re-alignment to match the practical skill needs of the Industry thereby enhancng core employability.

    CEEP Involves

  • Industry Realignment

  • Introduction to Industry Methodology

  • Engineering Systems Thinking (EQuad USP)

  • Industry Design Visualisation & Methodology

  • Real Time Industry Problem Solving

  • Engineering Project Handling & Management

  • Theory to Practise Application

  • Scientific Assessment & Personality Development

  • Transferable Technical Skills (Based on Industry Needs)

    • Small Module Training

    SMT ‘s are short 24 hour technical skill based modules available for students / fresh graduates and engineers from Industries – Large, Medium , Small or even Start-Up’s These short SMT’s provides much needed skill up gradation in order to stay relevant intune with the growing needs of the Industry SMT involves practical application, live examples, Do It Yourself Kits to experience Real time industry expectations on skill enhancement.

    Current SMT modules include

  • EST – Industrial Design Visualisation and Conceptualisation

  • EST - Approach to Industrial Circuit Design

  • EST – Industrial Route to Micro Controller Programming

  • EST – Robotics – Elements and Architecture

    Customised Technical Training & Recruitment

    EQuad unique CTTR – offers technically trained skilled engineers for Industry CTTR is a 12 week programme where initial 8 weeks (2 months) is spent at EQuad and the final 4 weeks at the client industry. The final 4 weeks will have EQuad monitor the candidate at the client industry

    CTTR ensures that

  • Industries receive only trained candidates

  • Candidates are far more productive on their immediate joining

  • Industry needs to spend as little as 100th the time in hiring the right candidate(s)

    Faculty Development Programmes

  • FDP’s are EQuad training offering to Faculty of Academic Institutions with special focus on Entrepreneurship & Engineering

  • FDP’s – held periodically or as one off training sessions would offer a synthesised & systematic approach towards entrepreneurship

  • The main objective of these FDP’s is to have faculty exposed to the realities that surround Entrepreneurship – both technical & non-technical aspects

    • Topics covered as FDP’s include (non-exhaustive)

  • Customer Centric Action Plan

  • Business Canvas Modelling

  • 5 W’s of the Business Plan –What, Who, When, Where & Why

  • Business Ideation & Winning Business Model

  • Systematic Entrepreneurship For a Winning Start-Up
